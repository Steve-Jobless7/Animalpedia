More about Amphibians!

Amphibians are a class of cold-blooded vertebrates known as Amphibia.They live both in water and on land during their life. They usually start as aquatic larvae with gills and later develop lungs and limbs to live on land. Their skin is moist and can absorb water and oxygen, and they typically lay eggs in water.

Poison Dart Frog

Brightly colored frogs from Central and South America. They produce toxins to deter predators.

Axolotl

Also known as the Mexican walking fish, axolotls are unique salamanders that retain juvenile features throughout life.

Tree Frog

Agile climbers with sticky pads, these frogs inhabit forests worldwide and are known for their vibrant colors and calls.

Eastern Salamander

Found in North America, these salamanders have smooth skin and spend much of their lives under logs and leaves.

Caecilian

Limbless, worm-like amphibians that live mostly underground in tropical regions. Rarely seen by humans.
