More about Reptiles!

Reptiles are cold-blooded vertebrates with scales that mostly live on land and breathe air through lungs. Most lay eggs, though some give birth to live young. Common reptiles include snakes, lizards, turtles, crocodiles, and tuataras.

Snakes

Legless reptiles with flexible bodies and scaly skin. Some are venomous, while others are constrictors.

Lizards

Reptiles with scaly skin, movable eyelids, and often long tails. Many can regrow lost tails.

Turtles

Reptiles with hard protective shells that live on land or in water.

Crocodiles

Large, powerful reptiles with strong jaws and tough, scaly skin.

Komodo Dragon

The largest living lizard in the world, known for its powerful bite and strong sense of smell.

Iguana

A large, mostly plant-eating lizard with a long tail and spines running down its back.
