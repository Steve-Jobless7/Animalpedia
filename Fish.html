More about fish!

Fish are aquatic animals that live in water and are usually cold-blooded. They have backbones, breathe through gills, and most are covered with scales and use fins to swim. Fish are classified into three main groups: jawless fish, cartilaginous fish (like sharks), and bony fish (like salmon). Most fish lay eggs, though some give live birth, and they are specially adapted to survive and move efficiently in water.

Salmon

A popular migratory fish known for its pink flesh and long journeys from ocean to freshwater to spawn.

Clownfish

A small, brightly colored saltwater fish famous for its symbiotic relationship with sea anemones.

Cod

A popular cold-water fish found in the North Atlantic, known for its mild flavor and flaky white flesh.
